WorkProof

On-device proof-of-work, anchored on-chain. Trust without surveillance.

02 / 08 Problem

Field work has no receipts.

Contractors, crews, and gig workers finish jobs every day — and can't prove it a week later. Photos scatter across camera rolls; disputes drag on; final payments stall.

03 / 08 How it works

Four steps. Zero servers.

A worker captures their work, the phone proves it, and the chain remembers it. No cloud middleman ever sees the content.

1

Capture

Open the WorkProof app. Snap a photo of the finished work, dictate a voice note, and stamp the location. Stays on device.

2

Summarize

On-device extraction turns the voice memo into a written report — work type, client, amounts, notes. Never leaves the phone.

3

Hash

SHA-256 of the summary + timestamp + signer key. The hash is the only artifact that ever leaves the device.

4

Anchor

Hash is written to Polygon Amoy. Anyone can verify: "this work existed at this time" — forever, for fractions of a cent.

04 / 08 Architecture

No backend. No middleman.

Every component lives at the edge or on a public chain. The trust surface is the smallest it can possibly be.

RN

RN Client

React Native app. Captures evidence, signs locally with secp256k1.

AI

On-device LLM

Llama 3.2 1B via llama.rn. Summarizes evidence offline.

#

SHA-256

Deterministic hash of summary, time, and signer pubkey.

⬢

Polygon Amoy

Cheap L2 anchor. ~$0.0001 per proof. Public, immutable.

05 / 08 Why it works

Three properties. No tradeoffs.

The architecture isn't clever — it's the minimum needed. Each property is necessary; together they're sufficient.

offline-first

Privacy by construction

Raw evidence — photo bytes, audio bytes — never leaves the phone. Only the SHA-256 hash goes on-chain. Optional Gemini extraction sees the typed transcript, not the media.

cryptographic

Tamper-evident

SHA-256 over the canonical bundle (fields + photo hash + audio hash) means any change to any input breaks the proof. Verification is public: recompute the hash, check it matches the anchor event on Polygon Amoy.

blockchain-anchored

Permanent timestamping

Polygon Amoy gives us a public, ordered record. "I did this work before X happened" becomes a fact anyone can verify, decades later.
